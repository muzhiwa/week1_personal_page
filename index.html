Germany

A Country In Europe

Germany, officially the Federal Republic of Germany, is a country in Central Europe. It lies between the Baltic Sea and the North Sea to the north and the Alps to the south. Its sixteen constituent states have a total population of over 84 million in an area of 357,596 km2 (138,069 sq mi), making it the most populous member state of the European Union. It borders Denmark to the north, Poland and the Czech Republic to the east, Austria and Switzerland to the south, and France, Luxembourg, Belgium, and the Netherlands to the west. The nation's capital and most populous city is Berlin and its main financial centre is Frankfurt; the largest urban area is the Ruhr. Settlement in the territory of modern Germany began in the Lower Paleolithic, with various tribes inhabiting it from the Neolithic onward, chiefly the Celts. Various Germanic tribes have inhabited the northern parts of modern Germany since classical antiquity. A region named Germania was documented before AD 100. In 962, the Kingdom of Germany formed the bulk of the Holy Roman Empire. During the 16th century, northern German regions became the centre of the Protestant Reformation. Following the Napoleonic Wars and the dissolution of the Holy Roman Empire in 1806, the German Confederation was formed in 1815.

Famous Cities

Berlin

Berlin, Germany’s capital, dates to the 13th century. Reminders of the city's turbulent 20th-century history include its Holocaust memorial and the Berlin Wall's graffitied remains. Divided during the Cold War, its 18th-century Brandenburg Gate has become a symbol of reunification. The city's also known for its art scene and modern landmarks like the gold-colored, swoop-roofed Berliner Philharmonie, built in 1963.

Frankfort

Frankfort is the capital city of the U.S. state of Kentucky. It is a home rule-class city and the seat of Franklin County in the Upland South. Popular German Foods

Schnitzel

Schnitzel (German: [ˈʃnɪt͡sl̩] ⓘ) is a thin slice of meat. The meat is usually thinned by pounding with a meat tenderizer. Most commonly, the meat is breaded before frying. Breaded schnitzel is popular in many countries and is made using veal, pork, chicken, mutton, beef, or turkey. Schnitzel originated as Wiener schnitzel and is very similar to other breaded meat dishes.

Kartoffelpuffer

Kartoffelpuffer are German potato pancakes, a popular dish in Germany and other parts of Europe. They are typically made from shredded raw potatoes, onions, eggs, and flour, then fried until golden brown and crispy. They are often served with applesauce or sour cream.

Currywurst

Currywurst is a popular German fast food dish, consisting of steamed, then fried sliced sausage (typically Bratwurst) topped with a curry-flavored ketchup or tomato-based sauce and sprinkled with curry powder. It's a staple in Germany, particularly in Berlin, where it's said to have originated in 1949.


Most Popular and Prestigious German Universities (Top Picks)

  • Technische Universität München (TUM)– Germany’s #1 technical university, strong in engineering, CS, and natural sciences.
  • Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität München (LMU Munich) – Top-ranked research university, especially in humanities, medicine, and social sciences.
  • Ruprecht-Karls-Universität Heidelberg (Heidelberg University) – Germany’s oldest university, famous for medicine, law, and philosophy.
  • Freie Universität Berlin (FU Berlin)– Modern, politically engaged, strong in international relations, social sciences, and humanities.
  • Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin– Historic, highly respected in arts, humanities, and political science.
  • RWTH Aachen University– Elite technical university, famous for mechanical and electrical engineering.
  • Karlsruher Institut für Technologie (KIT)– Germany’s MIT, leading in computer science and natural sciences.
  • Universität Mannheim – Known as the "Harvard of Germany" for economics, business, and social sciences.
